Self-Profile

Your Civic Involvement
  • I served from 1996-1999 with the United States Navy as an intelligence analyst in the Middle East.
  • I graduated from Waubonsee Community College (1999) and Illinois State University (2003)
  • I helped workers to organize a union at ATMI Precast as a volunteer organizer with Northern Illinois Jobs with Justice (NIJWJ) and provided community support when they were locked out, resulting in better paying jobs and benefits for nearly 100 workers.
  • I worked with NIJWJ again in (2014) to defeat an application by K12 Inc.(an 18-district virtual charter school) for operation in the Fox Valley. Virtual learning was later shown during the pandemic to be ineffective for most students.
  • I served on the East Aurora School Board from 2015-2019 where I was an early supporter of the historic move to provide busing for students and I worked to find a long-term superintendent after excessive turnover and two years of interim superintendents.
  • I became a founding member of Working Families Aurora when the current administration awarded $58 million in public funds to the Hollywood Casino $58M to facilitate their move from downtown to the interstate exchange at Farnsworth and I-88. We organized community members to speak out at city council meetings. Their objections were ignored, so I ran successfully for the position of Alderman-at-Large.
  • I worked with Working Families Aurora to get a referendum on the April 1st ballot asking voters whether they favored the adoption of strict campaign finance rules and the capping of political donations at $1,000 for any corporation doing business with the City of Aurora. Alderwoman Patty Smith challenged the referendum, and it was removed from the ballot. Unfortunately we didn't have the resources to defend its inclusion.
  • I am presently an active member of the Fox Valley Garden Club. I'm interested in developing a local food economy to help provide fresh, affordable food for our community.
  • As Alderman-at-Large I have consistently voted against pay-to-play practices and taxpayer-funded handouts to developers and to the politically-connected who donate to Richard Irvin's campaign.
Why Are You Running?

I'm running for Mayor of Aurora to advance an agenda for the people, which includes better jobs at a living wage, affordable single-family homes, addressing the climate crisis, and a strong administration that communicates openly and listens to residents. Aurora is a great and diverse city, but we need a strategic vision that carves out a future economy in the green/tech space instead of simply trying to be an entertainment destination.
I'm not running against any one individual, but, rather running to do my part to reform a broken political system that works incredibly well for connected and wealthy elites who buy off elected officials at every level of government with campaign contributions, and thereby enjoy preferential treatment on legislative issues. I don't take contributions from corporate PACs or people doing, or seeking to do business with the City of Aurora.

Chuck Anderson has represented the District 1 on the Fox Valley Park District Board of Commissioners for 10 years and has served as Board President for 8 of those years.
